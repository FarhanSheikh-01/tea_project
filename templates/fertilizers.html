Introduction

Fertilizers play a crucial role in maintaining the soil health and boosting the productivity of tea plantations. Different types of fertilizers are used to provide essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, ensuring healthy tea plant growth.

Common Fertilizers

  • Organic Manure: Composed of decomposed plant and animal matter, it improves soil texture and provides essential nutrients like nitrogen.
  • Urea: A nitrogen-rich fertilizer commonly used to promote healthy leaf growth and overall plant development.
  • Superphosphate: A source of phosphorus, which is essential for root development and flowering.
  • Potassium Nitrate: Provides both potassium and nitrogen, important for promoting disease resistance and enhancing the quality of tea leaves.

Fertilizer Application

Fertilizers are typically applied at different stages of tea plant growth. Organic fertilizers are often used during the initial growth phases, while chemical fertilizers such as urea and potassium nitrate are commonly used in established plantations to maintain soil fertility.
